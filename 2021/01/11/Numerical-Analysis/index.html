дёүз§ҚиЎЎйҮҸerrorзҡ„ж–№жі•

Absolute error : $|p-p^*|$

Relative error : $\frac {|p-p^*|}{|p|}$

Significant Digits:

$p^\star$ approximates $p$ to $t$ significant digits if the relative error is less than $5\cdot 10^{-t}$

дёҫдёӘдҫӢеӯҗ

Exact: 0.1

Approx: 0.099

жұӮ Significant Digits

йҰ–е…Ҳ relative error = 0.01

еҪ“ t = 0, relative error = 0.01 < $5 * 10^0$

еҪ“ t = 1, relative error = 0.01 < $5 * 10^{-1}$

еҪ“ t = 2, relative error = 0.01 < $5 * 10^{-2}$

еҪ“ t = 3, relative error = 0.01 > $5 * 10^{-3}$

еӣ жӯӨ t = 2. significant Digits = 2

Algorithms and Convergence

еҒҮи®ҫ sequence ${\alpha_n}$ converges to $\alpha$, еҸҜд»ҘеҶҷжҲҗ:
$$
\lim_{n\to \inf} \alpha_n = \alpha
$$
з„¶иҖҢжҲ‘д»¬дёҚд»…д»…е…ізі»$\alpha$, жҲ‘д»¬жӣҙе…іеҝғhow rapidly the sequence converges to its limit. (converge зҡ„йҖҹеәҰ)

algorithms that converge rapidly to their limit are going to be more efficient and more desirable to us


жқҘдёӘдҫӢеӯҗ:
$$
\alpha_n = \sin(\frac 1n)
$$
еҪ“ $n\to \inf$ , $\alpha_n$ converge to 0

$\alpha = \lim_{n \to \inf} \sin(\frac 1 n)=0$


з„¶иҖҢжңүж—¶еҖҷдҪҝз”Ё finite limit жҜ”дҪҝз”Ё infinite limit иҰҒз®ҖеҚ•

дҫӢеҰӮжҲ‘д»¬еҸҜд»Ҙиҝҷж ·жӣҝжҚў
$$
\lim_{n \to \inf} \sin(\frac 1n)
$$

$$
\lim_{h\to 0}\sin(h)
$$

дҪҝз”ЁhжқҘд»Јжӣҝ $\frac 1n$

$\lim_{h\to 0}\sin(h) = 0$


з„¶иҖҢжҲ‘д»¬жғізҹҘйҒ“ How rapidly sin(h) tends to 0.

дҪҝз”ЁTaylor series жқҘиҝӣиЎҢеұ•ејҖ.
$$
\sin(h) = h - \frac {h^3} {6} + \cdots
$$
еӣ жӯӨ
$$
\sin(h) - \lim_{h\to 0}\sin(h)
$$

$$
=\sin(h)-\alpha=\sin(h)-0
$$

$$
=h - \frac {h^3} {6} + \cdots
$$

иҝҷдёӘе‘ҠиҜүжҲ‘д»¬д»Җд№Ҳ?

as $h$ tends to zero $\sin(h)$ tends to zero and it does so in a similiar way to $h$

$\sin(h) \to 0$ like $h\to 0$
$$
h - \frac {h^3} {6} + \cdots
$$
дёәд»Җд№ҲжҲ‘д»¬еҝҪз•ҘдәҶе…¶дҪҷзҡ„й«ҳйҳ¶йЎ№?

еӣ дёәеҪ“hеҸҳе°Ҹж—¶ $h^3, h^7вҖҰ$ vanish very rapidly as $h$ tends to zero

еҪ“ $h\to 0$ е…¶д»–й«ҳйҳ¶йЎ№дјҡжҜ”hе°ҸеӨҡдәҶ

so the most important term here is the leading order term $h$, others are much much smaller.



$$ \lim_{n\to \inf}\alpha_n = \alpha $$ еҒҮи®ҫзҺ°еңЁжңүеҸҰдёҖдёӘ sequence $\{\beta_n\}$ $$ \lim_{n\to \inf}\beta_n = 0 $$ жҲ‘д»¬иҰҒе°ҶдәҢиҖ…е»әз«Ӣе…ізі». дёәд»Җд№Ҳ?

еӣ дёә$\alpha_n$ is something we want to understand.

иҖҢ $\beta_n$ is something we understand quit well


еҰӮжһң$|\alpha_n-\alpha| \le k|\beta_n|$, $k$ жҳҜдёҖдёӘеӨ§дәҺйӣ¶зҡ„еёёж•°

жҲ‘д»¬иҜҙ ${\alpha_n}$ converges to $\alpha$ with rate of convergence $O(\beta_n)$ (big-oh of $\beta_n$)

еҰӮжһң $\alpha_n \to \alpha$ with r.o.c (rate of convergence) $O(\beta_n)$ then we sometimes write
$$
\alpha_n = \alpha + O(\beta_n)
$$


иҖҢеңЁдёҠйқўзҡ„дҫӢеӯҗдёӯ
$$
\alpha_n = \sin(\frac 1n)
$$

$$
\alpha = \lim_{n\to \inf}\sin(\frac 1 n) = 0
$$

$\alpha_n = \sin(\frac 1n)$ was like $\frac 1n$ as $n\to \inf$

жүҖд»ҘжҲ‘д»¬еҸҜд»Ҙ compare with $\beta_n$ = $\frac 1 n$ (и®©$\beta_n = \frac 1 n$, иҝҷж ·е°ұеҸҜд»ҘиҝӣиЎҢжҜ”иҫғ)

$\alpha_n = \sin(\frac 1n)$ converges to $\alpha = 0$ with r.o.c $O(\beta_n) = \frac 1n$



йҖҡеёёжҲ‘д»¬compare how fast $\alpha_n \to 0$ with how fast $\beta_n=\frac 1{n^p} \to 0$

жҲ‘д»¬жғіиҰҒжңҖеӨ§зҡ„p, дҪҝеҫ—${\alpha_n}\to\alpha$ with r.o.c $O(\frac 1 {n^p})$



еҸҰдёҖдёӘдҫӢеӯҗ:
$$
\lim_{n\to \inf} n\sin(\frac 1n) = 1
$$
жҲ‘д»¬жғізҹҘйҒ“ how quickly it tends to 1.
$$
\alpha_n = n\sin(\frac 1n)
$$

$$
\alpha = 1
$$

и®© $h=\frac 1n$, дёҠйқўзҡ„й—®йўҳзӯүд»·дәҺиҝҷдёӘ
$$
\lim_{h\to 0} \frac 1h \sin(h) = 1
$$
so
$$
\frac 1h \sin(h) - 1 = O(h^p)
$$
е·Ұиҫ№еҸҜд»ҘеҜ№sin(h)иҝӣиЎҢжі°еӢ’еұ•ејҖ (еҜ№зӮ№0иҝӣиЎҢжі°еӢ’еұ•ејҖ, еӣ дёәlimи¶Ӣиҝ‘дәҺ0)
$$
\frac 1h \sin(h) - 1 = \frac 1h(h-\frac {h^3}{6}+\cdots) - 1
$$

$$
=1 - \frac {h^2}{6}+\cdots-1
$$

$$
=-\frac {h^2}{6}+\cdots
$$

жүҖд»Ҙ $\frac 1h \sin(h) - 1 \to 0$ like $-\frac {h^2}{6} \to 0$

rate of converges is $O(h^2)$ жҲ–иҖ…иҜҙ $O(\frac 1 {n^2})$
